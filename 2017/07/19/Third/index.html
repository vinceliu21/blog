Third

This is about to feel good.

My GPA was around a 3.986. I took 6 AP classes my Senior year. I got 5s on the AP Chem && Calc AB/BC tests without studying. I got into the college of my choice and I was ready to make an impact.

My a 1.86. I failed out of Calculus. I became entrapped in a hole that I thought was impossible to free myself from.

Man. What a difference half a year could make. In the span of only a few months, I went from a peak that I thought could only go higher to a moment of reflection where I wasn’t sure what had happened to my life. I am not looking for pity nor am I trying to brag. I am merely writing to share my story. Perhaps in hopes that it resonates with some who might be in similar situations.

In June of 2013, I graduated high school with my head held high. I was in the top percent of my class and never struggled with anything. The only B I ever got was Honors Biology freshman year. I held a plethora of leadership positions along with spreading myself across a majority of extracurricular activities. Later that same year in December – only 6 months later – I received an email from my university saying that my gpa had fallen below their academic probation line and if I don’t increase it the next semester, then I would be forced to leave for a year. Forced to leave??? That same year I thought I was at the top and now I was going to be FORCED to leave?

I became full of myself. And not the kind where I was bragging and showing off. No. The full of myself where I thought I could handle anything. I made myself into who I was in high school and thought I could do the same in college. I was that big fish in a little pond that I never told anyone about my grade situation, not even my mother.

That next semester, Freshman Spring, I managed to bring my overall GPA up to a 2.29. That was barely enough to just keep me in school. That was also one of the hardest semesters ever as I was trying just not to be kicked out. I wasn’t even thinking about A’s let alone B’s like I would have in high school. My mentality changed. For the next year or so, I could probably say I was a mess. My relationships deteriorated, I hit extreme bouts of depression, I sought therapy at my school’s counseling center, I isolated myself, but mainly I was just trying to figure out what the hell had happened. I slowly dissolved myself away from social circles and ended up in a hole.

In my period of reflection, plenty of thoughts had crossed my mind such as “Did I try to do too much?”, “Was I not seeking enough help?”, “Was university just too difficult for me?”, “Was I not working hard enough?”. I thought about everything, tried to remedy it all, and change anything I could. I blamed the world, everyone else, and myself.

I was wrong to do that though. I made a mistake.

Below you’ll find a graph of my GPA from freshman to senior year.

From an average of 2.29 my Freshman year, I just graduated from my university 3 months ago with an average GPA of 3.5 or better. I took harder classes, joined a sports team, found more extracurricular activities (BUGGY) for my fellow CMU readers.

Back then, I just hit a point where I didn’t believe anymore. I looked outwards for reasons to blame, but in reality I had the answer all along. It is fair to say that I had a tough transition from high school to college and that I was not hitting the books right. In all reality though, I hit a certain point of defeat where I believed I was not good enough.

For anyone out there in similar situations where maybe University seems too difficult or even life in general is hitting the rocks. Don’t give up. Keep believing in yourself and
