Trip

Road trip

So I’m going to talk about something I’ve always thought about doing, but never really knowing when I’d have the chance. I mean how often is it that someone actually takes a cross-country road trip?

Well it just happened to turn out that the perfect opportunity came up this past summer. I was about to finish up my spring term at CMU in Pittsburgh and start an internship at Samsung Research in California. Now instead of flying, I decided that this was the opportunity I was looking for and went all-in on this roadtrip!

This was the general path I decided to take.

I definitely wanted to stop in Chicago first because one of my close college friends who graduated a year prior was working there.

Not to mention there were the added benefits of Lake Michigan, the Chicago architecture, and a good old Chicago-style hot dog! We went to Portillo’s for the hot dog, which was pretty good. If anyone has any other recommendations for whenever I’m in the area then I’d be all ears.

Visiting Chicago for my first time, I planned to meet my friend at Millenium Park. It was both close to his work and contained landmarks that I’ve always to see in person. Albeit the day was a bit gloomy, the sights were still beautiful.

After I left Chicago, I began traveling west through a few states and it was in a small town on the border of Iowa and Nebraska where I experienced this sincere hospitality. I was coming up on the end of a long day’s drive when I arrived in Council Bluffs. I just needed a place to crash for the night and when I entered what looked like a building for truckers taking stops from their cross-country deliveries, I met a single woman at the counter.

The woman greeted me—or actually everyone—like we were a part of her family. A night when I thought I’d just grab something quick from a fast food joint, she prepared a home cooked meal for all the vistors staying in her miniature hotel.

This was the experience I was unconsiously looking for. Having stayed in the Philadelphia and Pittsburgh area my entire life, the Midwest is a part of the country I did not have much personal interaction with. Of course it was beautiful to see all the landmarks and changing scenary, but the random conversations and interactions with strangers are what made the trip for me. I suggest anyone reading this to travel somewhere they’ve never heard of. Experience the adventure of an unknown area and interact with the locals there. Meeting individuals from all different walks of life and getting to know their story is perhaps one of the greatest experiences.


I finished my trip traveling through the mountains of Colorado, salt flats of Utah, and beauty of California. I spent time to hike and explore the diverse aspects of nature. I was able to experience landscapes that are only unique to certain geographical areas.

Overall, although I took this trip by myself and had to start work in California, I highly recommend a cross-country road trip to anyone out there. And if that’s not to your liking then just travel to somewhere random and explore! The adventure and new experiences really made me appreciate taking this opportunity. I’ll be traveling to Japan soon so look out for an upcoming blog post about that! Also once again, feel free to reach out for any specifics about my journey across America!
