Japan

A Memorable Night

Ureshii (うれしい). The moment could have lasted forever and I don’t think I would have mind—five hours or so seemed to have blurred by in an instant. The bar staff slowly began to clean up and without even realizing until it was time to leave, we were the last ones there. It was the beauty of traveling—two groups of friends unknown to each other just hours ago and now intimately sharing parts of their life stories.

However, paying respect to the employees trying to close up for the night, we took our leave and walked outside.

The scene was absolutely beautiful—almost movie-esque in a way. The sun had begun to rise over Tokyo and in that moment I felt this unbelievable sense of being alive. All my senses seemed amplified, just the sound of the taxis and the brush of the wind was enough to make my hair stand. The moment could have lasted forever. Ureshii (うれしい)—a Japanese expression used as a term of happiness, a phrase I had learned from my new friends that night. And it was almost as if no one wanted the moment to end; we attempted to head to Tsukiji Fish Market, bodies exhausted and all, but the taxis refused a group of five people. I laughed.

Traveling lets you experience the World—the food, the culture, the scenary, but most important, the people. I am positive that my time in Japan probably still would have been amazing if we had not met Mai and Akari that night, but it was all that much better to make new friends.

So thank you Mai and Akari!

But most of important, while making new friends was definitely the highlight of the trip for me. Thank you to Justice and Ryan—two of the closest friends in my life that I got to experience this memorable trip with.

Pointers

For anyone thinking of heading to Japan, I would recommend a few things. First is to get a Japan Rail Pass before you leave. The rail pass will let you use the bullet trains between Tokyo, Kyoto, Osaka, and other cities without the need for purchasing anything over there. Also the price for a seven day pass is about equivalent to a round trip fare between Osaka and Tokyo. Next, I would recommend getting a pocket wifi—pretty much a consistent hotspot. You can reserve those before you head to Japan as well and pick them up at the airport.
